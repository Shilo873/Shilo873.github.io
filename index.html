Pratyush Parmar

Hello! I'm Pratyush Parmar, a passionate biotechnology student with a keen interest in exploring the intricate world of microorganisms and natural compounds. My academic journey has been enriched by hands-on experience in various fascinating projects that span across different domains of biotechnology.

Some Projects I've Worked On Include: Characterization of Promiscuous Plasmids From Natural Isolates of Lactic Acid Bacteria: In this project, I focused on identifying and characterizing plasmids from lactic acid bacteria isolated from natural sources. The aim was to understand the genetic elements that contribute to their adaptability and functionality, providing insights into their potential applications in biotechnology and medicine.

Isolation & Computational Analysis of Plant-Derived Compounds with Dual Antimicrobial and Anticancer Properties: I delved into the world of phytochemicals, isolating compounds from plants and analyzing their potential as both antimicrobial and anticancer agents. Utilizing computational tools, I was able to predict and validate the bioactivity of these compounds, contributing to the development of natural therapeutics.

Data Analysis of AQI of Rajasthan, India: This project involved the collection and analysis of air quality index (AQI) data from various regions in Rajasthan. By applying statistical and computational techniques, I was able to identify patterns and trends in air quality, providing valuable insights into environmental health and pollution control measures.

These projects have not only honed my technical skills but have also instilled in me a deeper appreciation for the vast potential of biotechnology in addressing real-world problems. I am always eager to learn, explore, and contribute to the scientific community, and I look forward to continuing my journey in this exciting field.
